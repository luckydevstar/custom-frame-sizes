Framing a jersey is part tailoring, part engineering. Fabrics stretch, sleeves fight you, and glittery numbers reflect weirdly under glass. Follow this sequence to avoid the two classic failures: **pressure marks from too-shallow depth** and **sloppy folds** that read amateur from five feet away.

![Measuring tape across jersey shoulder width inside planned shadowbox layout](/images/blog/frame-studio-hero.png)

## Step 1: Clean, dry, and stabilize

Work only on **clean hands/no food** surfaces. Lint-roll the interior. If the jersey was worn recently, ensure it is **fully dry**—trapped humidity inside a sealed package can lead to odor and mildew risk.

**Autographs?** Minimize handling of signed zones and plan folds so ink does not contact glazing or acidic mounts.

## Step 2: Choose flat vs mannequin-style presentation

Most home displays use a **flat mount** with strategic pinning or invisible stitching to hold shape. **Sewn-to-form** builds look dramatic but demand skill and more depth. If you are DIY-mounting for the first time, favor **flatter** compositions—you will get predictability.

## Step 3: Build the fold storyboard

On a large table:

1. Face the **front** outward—the spine of the garment defines your vertical.
2. Tuck sleeves **behind** the torso plane so thickness stays even.
3. Taper shoulder width until the silhouette **fits the opening** with at least **1/2″** breathing room to interior edges unless your design intentionally fills the window.

Tape a cellphone photo from the front to catch symmetry issues early.

## Step 4: Measure for depth honestly

Stack height is **fabric + folds + mount + any risers**. Add a little air—glazing should never kiss numbers or embroidery. If you are unsure, round **up** on depth; shallow shadowboxes are how mesh textures get **perm-creased**.

![Pins and clear monofilament on mat backing demonstrating textile mounting for display](/images/blog/botanical-frame-hero.png)

## Step 5: Mounting mechanics (beginner-friendly)

Common shop approaches:

- **Stainless/insect pins** into foam-core mounts (strategic, hidden behind layers).
- **Clear monofilament** tension lines stitched to mat board for sleeves.
- **Fabric-wrapped inserts** for color behind mesh windows.

Avoid **regular masking tape** on fabric long-term; adhesives can migrate and stain.

## Step 6: Glazing choice under lights

Numbers and patches are reflective—**UV protection** helps dyes and signatures; **anti-glare or acrylic** options can tame hot spots from track lighting. If the jersey hangs opposite a window, treat glare as a **first-class** requirement.

## Step 7: Seal, wire, and hang like heavy art

Assume **large shadowboxes exceed poster weight**. Use **robust wire or cleats**, hit studs or use appropriate anchors, and keep center of gravity low on the wall.

## Step 8: Photograph the mount before closing

If you ever need conservation help, an **interior photo** documents original pin positions and fold logic.

## When to hand off to a pro

Signed gamers, museum donors, or anything with sentimental panic value: a experienced textile framer earns their fee in **risk reduction**. If you are shopping online instead, supply **exact depth**, detailed photos, and **measurements of the folded silhouette** at its widest.

## UV, dyes, and locker-room realities

Modern synthetics resist fading better than vintage wool blends, but **autograph inks** and **heat-pressed names** still benefit from UV-smart glazing. If the jersey lived in **sun-blasted** display before you frame it, photograph any **uneven fading** so future conservators know it is pre-existing.

## Folding variants: vertical hero vs horizontal spread

Vertical compositions emphasize **numbers and crests**; horizontals celebrate **full torso width** and sleeve patch clusters. Neither is “correct”—choose based on wall height and viewing distance. Hallways love vertical; wide lounges tolerate horizontal **if** furniture does not crowd approach lines.

## Crash course in “don’t do this”

- **Tape directly to mesh** with aggressive adhesives.
- **Sew through** licensed patches without testing thread tension on heat-sealed layers.
- **Let metal badges** contact acrylic without micro-padding—scratch city.
- **Assume all jerseys share thickness**—goalie cuts and authentic on-ice builds vary enormously.

## Hand-off checklist for shops

1. Clean hands / gloves policy acknowledged
2. Photos: front, back, signature close-ups
3. Written fold approval or sample pin map
4. Final **max thickness** at thickest fold stack
5. Hardware expectations for destination wall type

## After delivery: acclimation and hanging day

Let packages **acclimate** to room temperature before opening if they have ridden cold trucks—condensation surprises cause panic wipes on glazing. Hang when you have **help** for wide shadowboxes; torque on one hanger corner invites racked joins.

## Long-term care

Dust the glazing on schedule, but **do not spray cleaners toward seams**—fluid ingress into fabric cavities causes stains that look like roster-number ghosts. Gentle is boring and correct.

## Variant builds: women’s cuts, junior sizes, and sleeves

Not all jerseys occupy the same **volume map**. Women’s tapered cuts can look crowded in stock openings; juniors may swim unless you design negative space intentionally. Sleeve battles are won with **tension balance**, not pinning aggression—let fabric talk before you commit holes.

## Documentation for teams and clubs

League fundraisers often need **matching shadowbox specs** across a roster wall. Standardize **depth and glazing** first so the wall reads cohesive even when jersey colors vary wildly.

## Patch swaps and career updates

Fans sometimes reframe when players **change numbers** mid-career. Design mounts that tolerate **re-pinning** if obsessive updates are likely—future you might thank past you for not supergluing hero moments permanently.

## Shipping jerseys to a remote framer

Roll **never** beats fold for signed kits headed through couriers—tube + acid-free tissue + desiccant awareness in humid months. Insure **replacement emotion**, not just market comps.
