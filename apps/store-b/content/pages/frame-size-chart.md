Standard frame sizes and what they're commonly used for. If your piece doesn't match a standard size, that's not a problem. Every frame we make is cut to your exact dimensions. Use this chart as a starting reference.

## Common Standard Frame Sizes

### Small Frames (4×6 to 8×10 Inches)

**4×6 inches**
- Standard photo print size
- Great for tabletop frames
- Ideal for snapshots and casual photos
- Fits standard 4×6 photo lab prints without trimming

**5×7 inches**
- Popular photo size
- Excellent for portraits
- Works well on desks and shelves
- Common for school photos

**8×10 inches**
- Most popular frame size
- Versatile for photos and art prints
- Accepts a 2-inch mat when framing a 5×7 print inside
- Standard portrait size

### Medium Frames (Ideal for Artwork and Larger Photos)

**11×14 inches**
- Common art print size
- Great for matted 8×10 photos
- Accommodates an 8×10 print with a standard 1.5-inch mat border
- Popular for certificates and diplomas

**12×16 inches**
- Good for larger art prints
- Works well for landscape photography
- Less common but increasingly popular
- Nice size for statement pieces

**16×20 inches**
- Large display size
- Excellent for poster prints
- Fits an 11×14 print with a 2.5-inch mat on each side
- Great focal point in a room

### Large Frames (Statement Pieces and Posters)

**18×24 inches**
- Standard poster size
- Impressive wall statement
- Common for art reproductions
- Holds a 16×20 photo with a 1-inch mat border all around

**20×24 inches**
- Large artwork display
- Professional presentation
- Good for gallery-style displays
- Works for oversized photography

**24×36 inches**
- Movie poster size
- Major wall statement
- Excellent for large art prints
- Requires substantial wall space

**27×40 inches**
- Full-size movie poster
- Dramatic wall piece
- Commercial art displays
- Collector's items and special pieces

## Using Mat Boards with Standard Sizes

Mat boards allow you to frame smaller artwork in larger standard frames. Common mat configurations:

**8×10 frame with mat**:
- 5×7 opening (2" mat on top/sides, 2.5" bottom)
- 4×6 opening (3" mat on all sides)

**11×14 frame with mat**:
- 8×10 opening (standard 2-2.5" mat)
- 5×7 opening (3-4" mat)

**16×20 frame with mat**:
- 11×14 opening (2.5-3" mat)
- 8×10 opening (4-5" mat for dramatic effect)

Learn more about mat selection in our [mat board guide](/mat-board-guide).

## When to Choose Standard vs. Custom Sizes

### Choose Standard Sizes When:

✓ Your artwork matches a common size exactly
✓ You want the most economical option
✓ You're framing mass-produced prints or posters
✓ You can trim your artwork slightly to fit
✓ You're creating a gallery wall with multiple frames
✓ You need frames quickly

### Choose Custom Sizes When:

✓ Your artwork is an irregular or non-standard size
✓ You're framing original artwork that can't be trimmed
✓ Standard sizes would require excessive matting
✓ You want exact proportions for a specific space
✓ You're framing valuable or irreplaceable pieces
✓ The artwork has specific dimensional requirements

## International Paper Sizes (ISO Standards)

If you're framing international prints or documents, be aware of these sizes:

**A-Series (Common in Europe and worldwide)**:
- A4: 8.3×11.7 inches (210×297mm)
- A3: 11.7×16.5 inches (297×420mm)
- A2: 16.5×23.4 inches (420×594mm)

These don't match US standard frame sizes, so custom framing may be necessary.

## Tips for Measuring and Selecting Sizes

1. **Measure your artwork accurately** using our [measuring guide](/how-to-measure)
2. **Consider the display location** - larger walls need larger frames
3. **Account for mat board** if you're adding matting
4. **Think about orientation** - portrait (vertical) vs. landscape (horizontal)
5. **Plan for groupings** - consistent sizes create cohesive [gallery walls](/gallery-wall-guide)

## Size Selection Based on Room and Purpose

### Bedroom
- Small to medium frames: 5×7", 8×10", 11×14"
- Personal photos and artwork
- Multiple frames or gallery walls work well

### Living Room
- Medium to large frames: 16×20", 18×24", 24×36"
- Statement pieces and focal points
- Consider furniture scale

### Office/Study
- Small to medium frames: 8×10", 11×14"
- Diplomas, certificates, inspirational quotes
- Professional presentation

### Hallway
- Varied sizes in gallery wall arrangement
- Or uniform sizes in linear arrangement
- Consider wall length and viewing distance

### Dining Room
- Medium to large frames: 16×20", 20×24"
- Artistic prints, family photos
- Consider as dining room focal point
